## Problem
Shopify store owners earning $10k–$100k/month struggle to stay competitive due to the lack of awareness about their competitors' pricing strategies. Manual monitoring of competitor prices is time-consuming and may lead to missed opportunities or lost sales.

## Target User
The target user is a Shopify store owner earning between $10,000 and $100,000 per month. They are likely to be interested in maintaining a competitive edge in the market and are willing to invest in tools that can help them achieve this goal.

## Solution
A lightweight SaaS that monitors up to 20 competitor URLs and emails a daily price delta report. The SaaS will provide the store owner with insights into price changes, enabling them to adjust their pricing strategy accordingly.

## Revenue Model
The revenue model for this SaaS is a monthly subscription. The subscription fee will be determined based on the number of competitor URLs being monitored, with discounts available for annual subscriptions or for monitoring a larger number of URLs.

## MVP Format
The Minimum Viable Product (MVP) will include the following features:
- Monitoring of up to 20 competitor URLs
- Daily email reports of price delta changes
- User dashboard for managing competitor URLs and subscription plans

## Estimated Build Time
The estimated build time for the MVP is 12 weeks, assuming a team of 2-3 developers with expertise in web scraping, data processing, and SaaS development.

## Validation Steps
Validation of the SaaS will involve the following steps:
- Conducting surveys and interviews with potential users to validate the problem and solution
- Creating a landing page to gauge interest in the SaaS and collect email addresses from potential users
- Launching a pilot version of the SaaS with a limited number of users to test the features and gather feedback

## Tech Stack
The tech stack for the SaaS will include:
- Frontend: React or Angular for building the user dashboard
- Backend: Node.js or Python for handling API requests and data processing
- Database: MongoDB or PostgreSQL for storing competitor URLs and price data
- Web scraping library: Scrapy or Beautiful Soup for monitoring competitor URLs
