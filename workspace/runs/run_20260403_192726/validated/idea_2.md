## Problem
Media organizations often struggle to find and retain diverse tech talent, which can lead to a lack of representation in their products and services. This can result in a limited understanding of the needs and experiences of underrepresented groups, ultimately affecting the organization's ability to create inclusive and engaging content.

## Target User
The target user is a diversity and inclusion manager at a media organization, responsible for ensuring that the company's workforce reflects the diversity of its audience. This user is likely to be interested in innovative solutions that can help them connect with underrepresented groups of engineers and provide training and development resources to promote diversity and inclusion.

## Solution
The Inclusive Tech Platform is a SaaS-based solution that connects media organizations with underrepresented groups of engineers, providing a range of training and development resources to support diversity and inclusion initiatives. The platform will offer features such as:

* A talent marketplace where companies can discover and connect with diverse tech talent
* A learning management system with courses and workshops on topics such as bias awareness, cultural competency, and inclusive design
* A community forum where users can share best practices, ask questions, and get support from peers and experts
* A resource library with articles, videos, and podcasts on diversity and inclusion topics

## Revenue Model
The Inclusive Tech Platform will generate revenue through a monthly SaaS subscription model, with pricing tiers based on the size and type of organization. Additional revenue streams may include sponsored content, advertising, and premium services such as customized training programs and diversity and inclusion consulting.

## MVP Format
The minimum viable product (MVP) will include a basic talent marketplace, a limited set of training resources, and a community forum. The MVP will be developed using a iterative and agile approach, with continuous user testing and feedback to inform the development of subsequent features and functionality.

## Estimated Build Time
The estimated build time for the MVP is 12 weeks, with a team of 4-6 developers, designers, and project managers. The subsequent phases of development will require additional time and resources, with a projected total development time of 6-9 months.

## Validation Steps
1. Conduct user research and surveys to validate the need for the platform and gather feedback on the proposed features and functionality.
2. Develop a prototype of the talent marketplace and training resources, and test it with a small group of users.
3. Launch the MVP and gather feedback from early adopters, using this feedback to inform the development of subsequent features and functionality.
4. Establish partnerships with organizations that serve underrepresented groups of engineers, to ensure that the platform is meeting the needs of its target users.

## Tech Stack
- Front-end framework: React
- Back-end framework: Node.js
- Database: MongoDB
- Cloud platform: AWS
- Payment gateway: Stripe
- Learning management system: Moodle
- Community forum: Discord
