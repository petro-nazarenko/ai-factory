## Problem
Shopify store owners earning between $10,000 and $100,000 per month struggle to stay competitive in their market. They need to constantly monitor their competitors' prices to adjust their own pricing strategy and stay ahead. However, manually tracking competitor prices is time-consuming and prone to errors.

## Target User
The target user is a Shopify store owner who earns between $10,000 and $100,000 per month. This user is likely to be interested in automation tools that can help them optimize their business operations and improve their competitive edge.

## Solution
Competitor Price Watch is a lightweight SaaS that monitors up to 20 competitor URLs and emails a daily price delta report to the user. This report highlights the changes in prices of specific products or product categories, allowing the user to adjust their pricing strategy accordingly.

## Revenue Model
The revenue model for Competitor Price Watch is a monthly SaaS subscription. Users can pay a fixed monthly fee to access the competitor price monitoring and reporting features. The subscription fee can be tiered based on the number of competitor URLs being monitored.

## MVP Format
The minimum viable product (MVP) for Competitor Price Watch includes the following features:
- Monitoring of up to 20 competitor URLs
- Daily price delta report via email
- Simple user interface for adding and managing competitor URLs
- Basic filtering and sorting options for the price delta report

## Estimated Build Time
The estimated build time for the MVP version of Competitor Price Watch is approximately 40 hours, assuming a team of one developer and one designer.

## Validation Steps
1. Conduct market research to validate the demand for a competitor price monitoring tool among Shopify store owners.
2. Reach out to potential users to gather feedback on the proposed features and pricing strategy.
3. Develop a landing page to gauge interest and collect email addresses from potential users.
4. Launch a beta version of the product and gather feedback from early adopters.

## Tech Stack
- Frontend: React or Angular for building the user interface
- Backend: Node.js or Python for handling API requests and processing competitor price data
- Database: MongoDB or PostgreSQL for storing competitor URL and price data
- Web scraping library: Cheerio or Scrapy for extracting price data from competitor websites
- Email service: Sendgrid or Mailgun for sending daily price delta reports to users
