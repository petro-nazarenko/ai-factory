## Problem
Shopify store owners earning $10k–$100k/month struggle to stay competitive, lacking the resources for comprehensive market research and price monitoring. This results in missed opportunities to adjust pricing strategies, potentially leading to lost sales and revenue.

## Target User
The target user is a Shopify store owner with a monthly revenue between $10,000 and $100,000. These users are established in the ecommerce space but may not have the budget or expertise for extensive market analysis and competitor price tracking.

## Solution
Competitor Price Watch is a lightweight SaaS solution designed to monitor up to 20 competitor URLs and provide daily price delta reports via email. This tool enables Shopify store owners to stay informed about price changes in their competitive landscape, allowing for more agile and data-driven pricing strategies.

## Revenue Model
The revenue model for Competitor Price Watch is based on a monthly SaaS subscription. The pricing will be tiered, with different plans offering varying numbers of competitor URL slots and additional features such as historical price data, alert thresholds, and integration with popular ecommerce platforms for seamless product catalog synchronization.

## MVP Format
The Minimum Viable Product (MVP) for Competitor Price Watch will include:
- The ability to input and monitor up to 5 competitor URLs.
- A daily email report showing price changes (deltas) for tracked products.
- Basic user dashboard for managing competitor URLs and subscription settings.

## Estimated Build Time
The estimated build time for the MVP is approximately 160 hours, considering the development of the core features, basic UI, and initial infrastructure setup.

## Validation Steps
1. Conduct surveys and interviews with potential users to validate the need for a lightweight competitor price monitoring solution.
2. Analyze existing solutions and their limitations to ensure Competitor Price Watch offers a unique value proposition.
3. Develop a landing page to gauge interest and collect email addresses from interested users.
4. Create a prototype or proof-of-concept to test core functionality and gather feedback from early adopters.

## Tech Stack
- Frontend: React or Vue.js for the user dashboard and settings interface.
- Backend: Node.js with Express for handling API requests, competitor URL management, and report generation.
- Database: MongoDB for storing user data, competitor URLs, and historical price information.
- Web Scraping Library: Puppeteer or Cheerio for extracting price data from competitor websites.
- Email Service: Sendgrid or Mailgun for sending daily price delta reports.
