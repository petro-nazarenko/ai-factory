## Problem
The process of finding qualified open-source software engineers for job openings can be time-consuming and inefficient for hiring managers. Traditional methods, such as searching resumes and interviewing candidates, often lead to suboptimal matches and lengthy hiring processes.

## Target User
Hiring managers at tech companies who are responsible for finding and hiring remote experienced open-source software engineers.

## Solution
The Automated Matching Platform AI will utilize machine learning algorithms to match job openings with open-source software engineers' skills and interests. The platform will consist of the following components:

1. **Job posting integration**: Connect with popular job boards and applicant tracking systems (ATS) to fetch job openings and relevant details.
2. **Engineer profiling**: Develop a comprehensive profile for each open-source software engineer based on their public online presence, which will include their skills, expertise, and past projects.
3. **Matching algorithm**: Utilize machine learning algorithms to identify the best matches between job openings and engineers based on their profiles, skills, and preferences.
4. **Notification and communication**: Send notifications to engineers about potential job matches, and facilitate communication between hiring managers and engineers.

## Revenue Model
The Automated Matching Platform AI will operate on a monthly SaaS subscription model. Hiring managers will be charged a fee based on the number of job openings they post on the platform, with discounts for long-term commitments.

## MVP Format
The minimum viable product (MVP) will include the following features:

1. **Basic job posting integration**: Connect with a single popular job board or ATS.
2. **Basic engineer profiling**: Profile engineers based on publicly available information.
3. **Simple matching algorithm**: Utilize a basic machine learning algorithm to match job openings with engineers.

## Estimated Build Time
6-9 months

## Validation Steps
1. Conduct market research to identify existing solutions and pain points.
2. Develop a pilot version of the platform with a limited number of users and job openings.
3. Gather feedback and iterate on the platform based on user feedback.

## Tech Stack
- Frontend: React.js for building the user interface.
- Backend: Node.js with Express.js for handling API requests and database interactions.
- Database: MongoDB for storing job postings, engineer profiles, and match data.
- Machine learning: TensorFlow.js and/or Scikit-learn for developing the matching algorithm.
